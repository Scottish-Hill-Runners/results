We've received news of a 15 hours 45 mins clockwise Tranter's Round completion in wintry conditions on 15th March 2016 by Mark Harris and Adam Harris, accompanied part way by Jon Gay and Al Anthony. A cracking effort which can't supplant the winter record through falling outwith the core, limited daylight, 'winter' months of December, January and February, but still shows what's possible with appropriate ground conditions.

Mark told us, 'We started out with Al and Jon. Jon returned at the beginning of the Grey Corries, and Al almost made it to the end, but cut down to Glen Nevis around the Devil's Ridge.'

Crampons and ice axes were used regularly, including a steep, icy ascent of Binnein Mor. Snow conditions were good above 700 metres... generally spring snow on south and west slopes and harder snow on eastern and northern slopes.
