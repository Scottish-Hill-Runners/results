---
title: "Records broken at Arrochar Alps"
date: 2012-07-10
excerpt: "Billy Bland’s 25 year old record at Arrochar was broken by 20 seconds on Saturday, and the ladies’ record went too.....  '"
---

Remarkably, given the deluge over the rest of the country, the clouds stayed clear of the summits for most of the day, and the ground conditions seemed no wetter than usual. This, together with a quality field from north and south of the border due to it being both a British and Scottish championship counter, undoubtedly helped with some remarkably fast times, with no fewer than 52 runners breaking the hallowed 4 hour mark....

The talk of the town at the finish was of an alternative route off Beinn Narnain which had previously been recced by the Borrowdale runners and was put to good use during the race, in particular by Ben Bardsley (also first V40) who broke Billy Bland’s long standing record by 20 seconds, a remarkable achievement. Joe Symonds was only 40 seconds behind, with Rob Jebb a further 2 minutes or so down.

In the ladies race, Jasmin Paris broke her own 2011 record by an equally amazing 17 minutes. She was followed by Helen Fines and Jeska Lauren, with Andrea Priestley first V40 in 4th, all coming in in under 4 hours.

Well done to Rob McQuater and his organising team and marshals for an excellent event, rounded off as usual by a great feed in the village hall.
