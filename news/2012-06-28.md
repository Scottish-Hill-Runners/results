_From Andrew Wright_

A bit of a dreich night for the Law Breaker this year with the mist engulfing the Ochils from 200 meters up and no clearance for the summit. Visibility remained fine though and temperatures were ideal for keeping cool on the brutal climb up The Law. With an ascent of 663 metres in only 2.7km followed by the leg burning descent it's a perfect Wednesday evening tester! Proving no slouch however was a resurgent Brian Marshall (HELP), who alongside 2nd placed Kenny Richmond (Shettleston Harriers) provided an entertaining sprint charge down the road to the the finish - one of many similar finales as runners came in. Young runner Adam Harris from Fife AC was a couple of minutes back in 3rd. In the senior females category Catriona Buchanan (Ochil HR) was well clear of Claire Gordon (HBT) with Jackie McIntyre (Carnegie Harriers) taking a creditable 3rd in her first ever hill race. Thankfully all runners managed back injury free despite the slippy conditions. A big thanks to all the marshals who helped out and suffered the midges, and to Mark Johnstone from Sweatshop Edinburgh for his generosity once again.
