The Lomond Hill Runners are delighted to add a midweek race to our portfolio that has been waiting to be done for a number of years.  The race starts and finishes at RSPB Loch Leven which is a fantastic facility we would like to make a donation to.  So for every runner, £3 of the entry fee goes directly to the RSPB Loch Leven which I'm sure you will appreciate.

It is a cracking route with two main climbs and fast running in between, and a typical Lomie descent to finish.  The route has fantastic views across Fife and Perthshire, and is very accessible from the M90.  

In conjunction with Scottish Hill Runners race insurance requirements, runners are expected to carry the standard kit, map and compass, as the weather can be absolutely anything in June, and it is exposed and potentially disorientating up top, though marshalls are at key locations with some waymarking.

As it is the first time holding the race, and we don't know the size of field, prizes are likely to cover your race costs and some wine!!

A route map and gpx file can be found below and on the races section of the Lomies website

[https://www.lomondhillrunners.co.uk/races.html](https://www.lomondhillrunners.co.uk/races.html)

If the race cannot take place on the date scheduled for reasons outwith our control, we will reschedule the event and carry your entry forward to the revised date.    
All entries are non-refundable.

The Organisers accept no responsibility for loss of clothing or equipment, but the car park is close to the start to keep your warm gear.
