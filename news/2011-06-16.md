Some of the favourites have only done 2 of the 3 races, so there could be some changes among the leaders before the championship has finished. However, there are also plenty of good runners who have done three, including both men's and women's leaders Matt Sullivan and Catriona Buchanan.

The Over 40s are being led by holder Veronique Oldham and Mark Harris. Both are starting to look unbeatable.

It looks like you have to be called Gordon to run well as an Over 50 as the leading 3 are all Gordon... Stewart, Cameron & Pryde (resp.). However the new upstarts into this class might cause an upset before the season is done. Holder Anita Hamilton is leading the female Over 50s. Former Ben Nevis record holder Ros Evans is looking unbeatable in the women's Over 60 and Martin Hulme leads the Men's Over 60.

With all their young guns, Shettleston are dominating the men's team championship, while with all their old fogies Carnethy look good for winning the Over 40 men's team. Carnethy are also leading both women's teams, but these are more open than the men's.
