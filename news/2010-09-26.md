_from organiser David Armitage:_

The new set of marker canes had a real test on their first outing with a blustery wet night on the hill but conditions on the day for the competitors were much more amenable on the 24th running of the Cairn William hill race.

There was very little between the first four as they tackled the two main hills of the race, but youth prevailed over experience on the flatter return section. Graham Bee (Fife AC) managed to stay clear of Robbie Paterson (Forres Harriers) to claim the overall winner’s trophy in his last race before a placement in New Zealand. Alec Keith (HBT) had taken the lead on the testing heathery descent but Graham Bartlett of Forres Harriers pulled away to retain the veteran men’s trophy. Ruth MacKenzie (Deeside Runners) had her usual strong descent to pull well clear of her rivals in the women’s race and retain the overall trophy. Cathy Wyse finished second with Anita Hamilton (Cosmic Hillbashers) in third.

Sam Burgess of Deeside Runners took two minutes off the previous mark to set a new male record of 23:07 in the tough 4.5km junior race, whilst Liam Clark shaved 15 seconds off the previous best in the 2km U12 race.

The full results, including split times for the various sections of the courses, are on the [Cosmics' web site](http://www.cosmics.org.uk/).
