You have the exciting chance to race to the top only, in this Saturday 11th May’s Glas Tulaichean at the Spittal of Glenshee. The 7.2km course climbs 670m to finish at the summit cairn, with the course record of 33.50 held by Bobby Quinn, Scotland’s most successful uphill racer, and the fastest female time of 38.50 set by former world champion, Angela Mudge.

Entry is on the day, and open to all athletes over 17, but the race’s profile is boosted this year as it forms the trial race for selecting Scottish teams for the British Athletics Senior Home International event (to be held in Keswick on 25th May). To be eligible for selection athletes must have been born in 1993 or earlier (full selection criteria are in the International Handbook).

The race organiser reminds all competitors that they **must** provide warm clothing to be taken to the summit, and that this must be handed in before 1.30pm, when entries close (the race starts at 2pm). In addition, there will be a kit check before the start and all competitors are required to have windproof clothing, map, compass and whistle. In the event of bad weather, which is forecast, waterproof body cover will be required and the route will be amended if the rivers cannot be crossed safely.

More event details and course map are [here](http://www.scottishhillracing.co.uk/RaceDetails.aspx?RaceID=RA-0054 )
