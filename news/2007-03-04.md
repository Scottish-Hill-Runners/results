title: "Marshall and Wardlaw win at Bishophill"
date: 2007-03-04
excerpt: "In the wind and rain at Bishop Hill, Brian Marshall (HELP) added yet another win to his tally, coming home in 18 min 47 s. Eilidh Wardlaw (HBT) won the women's race in 24 min 37 s."
---

Bishop Hill was in a miserable mood for the race on Sunday afternoon, with dark low cloud and a strong wind bringing the rain in bang on cue. The gliders stayed wrapped up at home, but 70-odd runners didn't.  
  
Brian Marshall (HELP) added yet another win to his tally, in 18 min 47 s. Dan Whitehead (Cosmics) was second, 41 s behind and just holding off Graeme Campbell (Highland Hill Runners) - another 10 metres and their positions would have been reversed. Alan Smith (Deeside) pipped Des Crowe (Shettleston) for the M40 prize; Brian Brennan (Westies) took the M50s and Tom Bowie (Fife) the M60s. The team title went to Ochils.  
  
Eilidh Wardlaw (HBT) won the women's race in 24 min 37 s from Kate Jenkins (Carnethy), who we're told had finished third in the Lasswade 10-mile road race less than two hours earlier. Judith Turner (Unatt) was third, with Joan Wilson (Carnethy) first F40 and Anne Nimmo (Carnethy) first F50.  
  
The first junior in the full race was Adam Harris (Fife) and the winner of the half-hill Jamie Crowe (Shettleston). But perhaps the most-deserved prizes of the afternoon (and the biggest cheer) went to the marshals.
