---
title: "Strain gathers in another title on Arthur's Seat, 26 July"
date: 2009-07-27
excerpt: "Murray Strain (HBT) and Susan Ridley (Edinburgh AC) take the main honours at Nairn's Hill Race, part of _The Gathering_.  '"
---

Nairn's Hill Race, part of _The Gathering_, was run over a testing two-lap course (7km, 450m ascent) on Arthur's Seat devised for the occasion by Martin Hyman.

After his recent exploits Murray Strain (HBT) was installed as the pre-race favourite. He lived up to that expectation with a comfortable win from Euan Jardine (Gala H) and Mark Johnston (Bellahouston RR) - who was probably relieved to have beaten his two brothers. Bruce Smith (Carnethy HRC) got the better of Jamie Thin (HBT) to be the first M40 and Gordon Pryde (Lomond HR) was the leading M50.

Susan Ridley (Edinburgh AC), twelfth overall, was well clear of the other women. The second and third places were taken by visitors: Hilary Ross from Newcastle and Paqui Villegas Navarro from Spain.

Retort of the day from a tail-ender about to start his second lap to the marshal telling him the route was the same as for the first one: "It was so long ago I've forgotten!".
