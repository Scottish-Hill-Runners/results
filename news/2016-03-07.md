Bishop Hill Race 06/03/2016

Once again fine weather held up for this year’s race. Frosty underfoot conditions on the hill eased just enough to allow some ‘give’ on the top surface making for secure footing. Only when everyone was going home did the snowflakes start to fall. Andrew Gilmore was a dominant winner with brother Iain gaining second place with a skilled descent. With John Hammond running strongly to take third, it was a 1,2,3 for Carnethy. Brian Marshall (HELP) was first vet in fourth place, with Alan Smith (Deeside) taking the over 50 prize. A close battle for the over 60 honours saw Brian Brennan (Westies) pip Pat Fitzpatrick (Ochil) by one second! Bob Beveridge (Lomond) was over 70 winner, one of four men over 70 running on the day.

Jill Stephen of HBT was the first lady and veteran Mary Lye (Carnethy) second. Third was Lisa Allan from Cairngorm Runners. Joan Wilson (Lomond) and Jocelyn Scott (Fife AC) were over 50 and over 60 winners.

Under 18 winners in the main race were Calum McTavish (Lomond) and Laura Nicoll (Tayside Orienteers).

There was a junior race to halfway and back. Finlay Ross (Harmeny) held off Struan Bennet by a mere second after a close tussle all the way. Ailsa Bennet won the girls race with Eva Hurrel second.

Thanks again to the marshals and the makers of soup, scones etc. Several people offered help on the day which made for a good atmosphere.

Richard Gatehouse (Race Organiser)
