The Tomnabat Hill Race at the Tomintoul Highland Games was the second short race in this year's SHR Championship.

_Tilly Smith reports:_

For a race that normally only attracts about 20 runners the Tomintoul Highland Games were delighted to see 118 runners setting off round the track and up Tomnabat. Although rough going it was dry under foot. The race became a family affair with Dan and Clare Whitehead both breaking the course records, Dan by nearly a minute and Clare by an amazing 6 minutes. Generous cash prizes were provided by the Games Committee for all categories and the day was completed with many runners heading for a hog roast at Alan and Tilly Smith's farm.

It was good to stage a Championship race at a Highland Games, it added another dimension to the day, provided extra entertainment for runners' families and of course the local community of Tomintoul benefitted from the extra admissions to the Games and more folk in the village.
