---
title: "Creag Choinnich Hill Race Results"
date: 2016-06-23
excerpt: "Wins for James Espie (Deeside) and Claire Green (Pudsey & Bramley) at the race on Wednesday 22nd June"
---

The Creag Choinnich hill race - the worlds oldest recorded hill race - took place on Wednesday 22nd June, at a new time and venue, as an evening race from Braemar Castle.

Race winner was James Espie (Deeside Runners) 17:24 and the first female was Claire Green 21:37 (Pudsey and Bramley AC)

Twenty five runners competed in total on a fine and bright evening. The route was mostly on technical single track trails.

In a nod to the races history, the runners were piped to the start line and greeted at the top of the hill by locals dressed in Jacobite costume bearing a standard. Post race refreshments were in the grand setting of Braemar castle dining room.

  

Ali Hubbard (race organiser)
