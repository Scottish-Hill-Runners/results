Andy Symonds has leapt to the top of the [Long Classics League Table](http://chris-upson.com/LongClassics/2009/LongClassics2009_v10.xls) following a strong run at Two Breweries.

With one race remaining - Pentland Skyline on 11th October - the only runner who could theoretically catch Andy is Tom Owens.

Alec Keith drops to second place, and Stewart Whitlie in third place has moved up to within a single point of Alec with a good run at Two Breweries.

The male vet 50 competition is currently lead by David Dickson of Garscube, but will be a close finish with Russell Anderson of Moorfoots if Russell runs the final race.

Graham Arthur has completed the most races with 9 out of 10, and has a comfortable lead over Charlie Love in the male vet 60 competition.

Ellen Homewood continues to lead the women's competition, although Susannah Macmillan has closed the gap enough to put the pressure on for the final race. Jacqui Higginbottom and Alayne Finlay are currently sitting in 3rd and 4th positions.

Elaine Stewart has won the female vet 50 competition for the 2nd year in a row.
