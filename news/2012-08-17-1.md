It rained rather heavily during registration, but then it pretty much stopped and Turnhouse re-appeared out of the clouds in time for the start, which was nice.

The going on several of the small paths used by the route seemed for some reason slower than in previous years and the results backed this up.  
Though to be honest, after running Sierre Zinal last weekend, Murray Strain was not looking to break any records tonight. Instead he was looking over his shoulder to make sure he finished just ahead of Don Naylor without doing anything extra. Rounding off the top 3 was Jacob Adkin, whilst sister Scout the first woman.

Numbers were lower than usual possibly because of the postponement from July, also a large contingent of Carnethies were up the road at Carlops.

Callum Campbell won the junior race ahead of younger brother Fergus.

I happen to think this is a cracking little route for a race, thanks everyone who came, I hope you enjoyed it.

Andrew Kitchin
