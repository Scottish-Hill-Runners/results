---
title: "Ben Venue Race Report"
date: 2015-10-07
excerpt: "Race report from the organiser, Andy Birnie, to follow the results published here a couple of days ago."
---

A small but perfectly formed band of runners enjoyed superb conditions on Ben Venue on Saturday. With some of the driest underfoot conditions ever seen on the ridge, it made for fast racing. After an outstanding descent, young James Elspie from Deeside Runners, came out on top in his raid from the north. He was quickly followed by Steve Feltbower (Ochils) and Thomas Knox (Anster Haddies), not far ahead of his dad, Jonny, who was first vet. The ladies race was won by Christina Rankin (Kilbarchan), ahead of 1st vet Val Houston (Westies).

The race was rounded off in lovely afternoon sunshine enjoying the fantastic catering from hosts, Bellahouston Roadrunners. On behalf of Lomond Mountain Rescue Team, I would like to thank all racers & supporters, who helped raise £500 for their funds.

Lomond MRT had a busy day on Saturday. It started with a morning training exercise before marshalling for the race. During the race, the team was called to deal with a broken ankle on Ben Lomond, so 10 team members raced off to deal with that. At 7pm, the team was called out again to deal with an injured walker high on Ptarmigan ridge on Ben Lomond, then before that evacuation was complete, callout #3 came in to help in the search for a missing person near Strathblane. Three successful callouts for the team, but they didn’t get home till the early hours of Sunday morning.
