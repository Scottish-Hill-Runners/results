Carnethy's James Waldie takes the honours at Norman's Law

From organiser Mark Harris:

James Waldie (Carnethy), who lead for most of the race, won a rainy Norman's Law. He was closely followed by Chris Russell and Adam Harris (both Fife AC). The women's race was won by Halina Rees with Alison McGill and Rhona van Rensburg (all Fife AC) in second and third. First male junior was Alaistair Robertson (Lothian, wrongly announced at the prize giving, for which apologies). The race began with a minute of silence in memory of Alan Milligan, international hill runner, who died last year. A new trophy has been set up in his honour to be presented to the first Fife AC runner, which this year was Chris Russell.

The Emily Hill race was very well attended despite the rain. It was won by Tristan Rees (Fife AC) for the boys and by Laura Nicoll for the girls (unattached).

Many thanks to all, especially the volunteers.
