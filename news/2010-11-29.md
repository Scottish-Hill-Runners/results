---

_From organiser Peter Baxter:_

Despite the snow all around us the decision was to go ahead with the race as we only had a few inches in Peebles. In consultation with FE we decided to lower the courses to the Red Squirrel for safety, the criteria being we had to get a 4x4 to the top of the courses.

Apologies to those who could not make it due to the weather... there are some things even the PCC team cannot control. We will keep your Xmas puddings to the next race!

The race itself was held in glorious sunshine, despite the sub-zero temperatures. A full team of marshals, two 4x4s and a couple of sweepers kept everyone on course and safe. Carl and team provided hot soup etc. to finishers providing a welcome warm-up.

Despite trailing one of the youngsters on the bike Euan Taylor stormed through on the run to take first place overall. Kerry MacPhee had a great race to finish in 8th position overall, from 14th on the bike.

An enormous amount of hard work was put in by all concerned to make this a successful first race in the series.

Here's hoping we get the sunshine without the snow, lovely as it was, for the next races.
