We're well into April, the days are lengthening and the season for evening races has been opened by the Whangie Whizz. (It's also the first in the [Bog and Burn Series](Championship.aspx?ChampionshipID=C-003).) Here's race Organiser Dave Calder's report:

"112 runners competed in this year's Whangie Whizz, down a few on the last couple of years. Perhaps a few were put off by the earlier weather; at 3 in the afternoon the omens were not good with heavy rain looking set for the day. By the race start though, the skies were clear and the runners set off in brilliant sunshine. The earlier rainfall ensured that this bog and burn event lived up to the description. The men's race was won by Nick Barrable of Tonbridge AC, who turned up only a couple of minutes before the start, in a time of 29:06. Runner up was Jamie Stevenson of Ochils in 29:41 and third place was taken by Bob Wiseman, also of Ochils, in 29:50.

Less surprising was Angela Mudge's victory in the women's race in 32:44. There was a great tussle at the end for 2nd and 3rd place between Iona Robertson of Bellahouston and Ellie Homewood of Westerlands, with Iona just edging ahead.

All-in-all a fine start to the Bog and Burn series with a few hillrunning virgins taking part."
