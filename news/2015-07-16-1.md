---
title: "Mark Harris Breaks Tranter Round Record !"
date: 2015-07-16
excerpt: "Mark's run of 11 hrs 31 mins beats previous record of 12hrs 17 mins held by Stephen Pyke. Mark's Report -"
---

On Wednesday 15th July at 7.31am I left the Youth Hostel in Glen Nevis to attempt an anti-clockwise Philip Tranter Round, which takes in most of hills in the Charlie Ramsay Round. Running conditions were perfect - sun and some high clouds, cool, light wind. Jon Gay accompanied me to Sgurr a’Mhaim, at the end of the Devil’s ridge. From there I was on my own to Stob Ban where my son Adam met me. Breaking the record seemed possible when I crossed the Abhainn Rath in exactly 6 hours, despite some small navigational errors. Some of these hills were unfamiliar to me but with clear vision of the whole route it was a case of getting good lines to speed up the footing. We got to the top of Aonach Mor with 2 hours to achieve a sub 12 hour time. The chicken runs beneath the CMD arete speeded up the traverse. For once I enjoyed the descent off the Ben with a pale gold Loch Linnhe in the evening sunshine to distract my concentration. And at 7.02pm I returned to the Youth hostel after 11 hours and 31 minutes of running. That means I broke Stephen Pyke’s record of 12 hrs 17 mins by 46 minutes. Mark Harris
