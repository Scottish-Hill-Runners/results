Report from race organiser, Sean O'Sullivan:

"The inaugural Grampian Mountain Challenge was a great success. Over 70 runners beat their way through a strenuous orienteering course, navigated over the snow topped Deeside Corbett, Cullardoch, and down to the mid-camp at the former Queen's Shooting Lodge, Corndavon. After an evening of beer, hot food (provided), traditional music and heat from a massive gas burner, everyone settled in satisfied for the night, but woke to find more snow on the hills!

Sunday gave the same interest, will hill first, then orienteering. Duncan Grassie slipped up on the hill losing 20 minutes at one control, while Kev Harper's sore feet saw his 1st place on Saturday's orienteering fall to 11th on Sunday. In the end, Tom Owens and Oleg Chepelin swapped positions with Alec Keith, to become the first ever GMC champions. Gareth Yardley, and Robin Orr/James Kellock were overall leaders of the B class and C class, respectively."
