Looking up at the hills from race registration in Killin, the mist didn't look like it was going to shift, but at the race start it had reduced to a few clouds drifting around the corries and summits. A good turnout saw 74 runners completing the course, with George Foster of Dundee Hawkhill taking first place and Bob Wiseman of Ochil Hill Runners a close second. It was great to see Angela Mudge racing again, and true to form she was first female. Diane Baum of Lochaber just managed to overhaul Jill Stephen of HBT on the punishing cobbled track that leads to the finish. A few bloodied knees were in evidence at the end on victims of the cobbles!

A good number of runners came back for refreshments and prizes down in Killin, and we raised well over 600 to split between the National Trust for Scotland towards conservation work at Ben Lawers NNR and the Killin Mountain Rescue Team, who did a great job marshalling on the hill. Thanks as well to the many local volunteers who helped with registration, time keeping, car parking and refreshments. More thanks to Killin Outdoor Centre, Salomon and Killin Co-op for all the donated prizes.

Dan Watson
