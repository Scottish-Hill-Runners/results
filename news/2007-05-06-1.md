The first counter in this year’s Scottish Hill Runners’ Championship drew nearly 200 runners to Strathyre, where Archie Bennie and his team staged their usual immaculate and much-appreciated race.

Simon Peachey (Carnethy) threw down the gauntlet from the start and was still leading when he crossed through Glen Ample for the second time, with Brian Marshall (HELP) loping along about 100m behind and Stewart Whitlie (Carnethy), head determinedly down, a further 200m back. It was Whitlie who finished the strongest to win the race for the fourth time in succession, just 16 seconds ahead of Peachey and a minute in front of Marshall. Edinburgh University’s [British Junior Orienteering Champion](http://www.scottish-orienteering.org/soa/index.html) Duncan Coombs gained several places in the second half of the race to claim fourth, with Colin Donnelly in fifth the first of a strong Lochaber squad.

Jill Mykura (Carnethy) comfortably won the women’s race, but there was a great battle for second, Hazel Dean (Central) holding off Dawn Scott (Lochaber) and taking the F40 prize as well.

There’ll be more from our statistician when he’s had time to pore over the results.
