From Mike Lamont (Moorfoot R):

A small but perfectly-formed group of souls braved the soggy slopes of Lee Pen on 19 July. The race is part of the St Ronan's Games, the oldest organised sports meeting in Scotland - started in 1827 by, amongst others, James Hogg, 'the Ettrick Shepherd'.

The race started with a loop of Vale of Leithen football ground, then out along a few footpaths and up the road to St Ronan's Well. From there it winds its way through the woods and then out on to the grassy, bracken-covered slopes of Lee Pen. The route cuts a diagonal course up the side of the hill then levels off slightly, before the steep, rocky ascent to the top. It's then back the way you came.

Craig Mattocks from Northants, who had won the St Mary's Loch durty Triathlon last month, was leading most of the way. Apparently though, he took a wrong turning in the woods on the way back and was beaten by Fife's Chris Russell in a time of 21:41. Moorfoots' Darin Dougal and Wull Hynd took third and fourth, respectively. Kate Jenkins was first lady in 33:16.
