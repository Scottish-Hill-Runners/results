Just over 100 runners turned out for the first of the long counters for the SHR championship with 87 runners completing the full course of nine Munros around the Glenshee ski centre. Conditions were generally favourable for the lead runners, but the weather deteriorated to heavy showers for the later finishers.

Graham Gristwood led the field home in a strong time of 3:10:41. His split at the road crossing was up on the current record but he was unable to sustain the pace over the final three summits. Alasdair Anthony was second in a time of 3:21:43 and Dan Gay next in 3:22:34.

Jasmin Paris put in an outstanding performance, smashing the female record by nearly twenty minutes. Her time of 3:37:17 gave her a position of 7th overall. Fanny Gyurko was second female in just over four hours with Stephanie Provan hot on her heels in a time of 4:01:59.

Other records to fall were the MV40 by Kenny Richmond, 4th overall in a time of 3:24:31. Val Houston knocked half an hour off the FV50 record with a time of 4:52.07 and a new benchmark for the MV70 was set by Gareth Byran-Jones in a time of 5:34:48.

Special thanks to all the members of Braemar MRT who marshalled and to Glenshee staff who hosted the event and provided race refreshments.

Ali Hubbard
