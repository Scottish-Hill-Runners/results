_From race organiser David Rogers:_

Wednesday saw perfect conditions and a big turnout for the Cort-ma Law race, with 99 runners completing the course and enough wind to make life pleasant everywhere except in the midge-infested hell-hole around the stream crossing. Kenny Richmond (Bellahouston) won the race for the second year in a row, but with a time of 48 minutes and 35 seconds did not quite reach his own record time of 48:16 from the previous year. Fiona Maxwell (Shettleston) was first woman in 54 minutes and 15 seconds and 15th place overall. Carnethy won the male team prize and Westerlands the female. Deborah Stokoe (Westies) was the first (and only) junior in 1:15:35.

Thanks to Lomond Mountain Rescue Team and Westerlands colleagues for logistical support.
