---
title: "World Trophy becomes World Championship - but Scotland loses out"
date: 2008-10-01
excerpt: "The WMRA World Trophy has been given World Championship status - but Scotland will no longer be able to enter it as an independent nation."
---

At its Annual Congress last month the World Mountain Running Association agreed that from 2009 the World Trophy be upgraded to a full-blown World Championship. That is good news for the sport globally.

The considerable downside from our point of view is that Scotland will no longer be allowed to send its own teams to the World Championship, senior or junior; and neither will the other Home Nations. They will be replaced by teams representing Great Britain and Northern Ireland. (This is already what happens in the European Championships.)

You can read [here](http://www.scottishathletics.org.uk/index.php?p=181&itemType=news&itemId=3763) the reflections of David Armitage, the Convenor of **scottish**athletics' Hill Running Commission.
