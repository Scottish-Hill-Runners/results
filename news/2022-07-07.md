That's four of the six races done, but no short races yet, so everything still to play for as all the favourites still have to turn up to at least one of the last two races - that's if you are a youngster (i.e. under 60!). Due to the rules for the older categories, we actually have winners already in the new Over 65 category, these being Jocelyn Scott (Fife) and Roderick Pugh (Kilmarnock), who are both unbeaten and have contested all four races so far - congratulations both (and both deserve it being well known supporters of the SHR for many years).

Because of the way the races have fallen, if I mention any favourities, there is no guarantee they will even finish the championship, so I will leave you to work out who can win.

In the teams, Carnethy HR are looking good in both men’s teams category, while Highland HR and Fife AC are similarly top of the women’s Senior and Over 40s categories resp.

If you have done all four races so far and think your points total is incorrect, remember only your best three results are counted, as you need a short as your fourth race (except in Over 60+ categories).
