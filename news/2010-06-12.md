Tom Owens (Shettleston) won the Durisdeer Hill Race by more than 12 minutes from Dave Eiser (Ochils), with Alan Smith 3rd. Natalie White (Kendal) was first lady followed by Alayne Finlay (Ochils) and Julia Connor (Moorfoot).

_From race organiser Adam Anderson:_

"Thirty-four runners entered the race in near perfect weather conditions. This is a record entry for a non-championship year in one of the busiest race Saturdays of the year.

The course was in fine condition and as always a few are caught out by the steepness and at times ruggedness of the apparent rolling grass covered hills of The Lowthers. Tom Owens of Shettleston had no problems and left the field in his wake winning by 12 minutes. Natalie White of Kendal was first female by a similar margin.

The catering department won the day providing a seemingly endless supply of tea n goodies. Thanks as always to Moffat Mountain Rescue, volunteer marshals and officials."
