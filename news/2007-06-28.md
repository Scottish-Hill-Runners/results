---
title: "Whitlie and Scott win inaugural South Black Hill Race, Pentlands, 27 June"
date: 2007-06-28
excerpt: "The race is the first of the three-race Pentland Ridge series.  '"
---

The nip from the jaws of this new route was the setting sun, which made it difficult to pick out the flags marking the surprisingly-long climb up through the heather of South Black Hill. The sting in its tail was the descent through the quarry – which runners were told they could by-pass if they wished, on payment of a time penalty.

Stewart Whitlie (Carnethy) coped with everything in his usual efficient way, to win from Steven Fallon (Carnethy) and Des Crowe (Shettleston). Elspeth Scott (Westerlands) emerged somewhat stung from the quarry but nevertheless comfortably ahead of Kate Friend (Carnethy) to be the first woman home.

The other two races the series are Carnethy Hill on 11 July and Turnhouse Hill on 25 July, each with a junior race.
