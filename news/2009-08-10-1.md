---
title: "Tom Owens bags the Glenshee 9 in record time, 9 August"
date: 2009-08-10
excerpt: "Tom Owens (Shettleston H) won the Glenshee 9 in record time. Gill Irvine (Highland HR) was the first woman.  '"
---

On a fine day for a long race, 69 runners lined up at the Glenshee ski centre for the gruelling 34km long Glenshee 9 race. Despite the forecast of threat of afternoon showers, the weather stayed fair and with the ground relatively dry, conditions were pretty much ideal.

From the off, Tom Owens (Shettleston H) and Alec Keith (HBT) set a cracking pace, with the field behind stretching out quickly. Also keeping very close to the front was a visiting Canadian running his first ever Scottish hill race - in trainers! - and ending up in a very commendable 6th position.

Tom and Alec were still together after the climb up to Carn Aosda from the road crossing, but Tom then pulled away comfortably to finish in a very impressive time of just over 3h 20min, six minutes inside last year's inaugural record. Alec finished second overall and 1st V40, also just inside the old record, whilst Dave Cummins (Shettleston H) enjoyed a strong second half of the race to pull through to third place. David Scott (Ochil) had another fine run to be 1st V50, finishing just 8 seconds under the 4hr mark.

Gill Irvine (Highland HR) was first woman in 4:32, with Ellie Homewood (Westies) second in 4:40 and Louise Burt (Fife AC) third.

Welcome refreshments were available at the Glenshee ski centre afterwards and once again the whole race was excellently organised and supported by members of Braemar Mountain Rescue Team.
