_From organiser Louise Kyle:_

The Cairn Table Run, organised by East Ayrshire Councils’ Countryside Services Department for the past 4 years, took place this year on Saturday 12th June. Cairn Table is one of the highest hills in Ayrshire, rising to a height of almost 600 metres, and runners from various clubs across Scotland entered this year’s 6 mile race.

With a record of 31.08 which was set by Tom Owens of Shettleston Harriers in 2009 to beat, 24 runners took off hoping to beat his record. Ducrot Mayeul from Ayr Seaforth took first place in 32.40 minutes meaning Tom Owens’s record remains intact for another year. Ducrot was followed by Don Reid of Westerlands with a time of 34.41 with Alan Tait from Motherwell AC taking third place in 35.40.

Toni McIntosh from Ayr Seaforth took first place for the women in a time of 40.25, 13 seconds inside the record set in 2006 by Jo Whitehead. She was followed by Gayle Tait of Motherwell AC in a time of 43.17. Gael Riddle finished in third place in 44.34.

Well done to everyone who took part.
