The SiEntries link for the Greenamantle Dash race on Thursday 2nd January is now open - [SiEntries - Greenmantle Dash](https://www.sientries.co.uk/event.php?event_id=14665)

The Greenmantle Dash (3.2km, 200 metres) is a short hill race packed with a bit of everything - scaling walls, wading burns, traversing bogs, farm fields, steep ascents and descents, as well as a final road sprint to the finish. Hill shoes are however advised!!

The kids' fun run initially follows the main race route (with alternative start to avoid the burn crossing if burn is in spate), with the turn-around point just before the main climb starts (ca. 2.3km, 50 metres).

The race starts in front of Broughton Ales brewery.

Start Time:

*   12pm (senior 14+);
*   12.05pm (kids' fun run: under 8-year-olds must be accompanied by an adult - enter on the day)

Registration from 10.30am at Broughton Village Hall (ML12 6HG). There will be entries on the day.

**Parking**

For environmental reasons and limited parking, please car-share as much as possible. Parking is possible at the back of the village hall and Broughton High School. Avoid parking elsewhere in the village so that we don't create any obstructions for the local residents.

**Facilities**

There are toilets and changing facilities in the village hall and public toilets in the park close to the start.

**Safety and rules**

This race is run under SHR rules (more details here [https://www.scottishhillrunners.uk/Safety.aspx](https://www.scottishhillrunners.uk/Safety.aspx)) and although it’s a short, well-marshalled race, the weather conditions can be rather grim in January. Therefore, you must carry the following minimum kit: waterproof full body cover, hat, gloves, map, compass and whistle. If you retire from the race, please inform the nearest marshal and return your number to the finish (race organiser).

**After the race**

There will be a get together in the village hall after the race, for the prize giving and hot soup/ drinks. PLEASE bring your own re-usable cup and cutlery to reduce waste.

More information can be found at [https://penicuikharriers.org.uk/greenmantle-dash/](https://penicuikharriers.org.uk/greenmantle-dash/)
