_From Dave Scott:_

A full complement of sixteen hill runners from clubs in the Borders, Central and Fife successfully completed the first SHR-organised navigation course in Peebles at the weekend. The Peebles Community Centre proved an excellent venue for the indoor sessions, which included map interpretation, compass skills and race planning. On Saturday afternoon the course took to nearby Kailzie Hill to apply skills learned in the morning, whilst on Sunday afternoon participants formed pairs to follow an orienteering-style course on the heather clad Windlestraw Law in the Moorfoot Hills, which all managed to navigate without too many omissions! Hill vocabulary however proved a challenge for some, who hopefully now know the meaning of “col”!

Many thanks to all participants for supporting the course and to instructor Bruce Poll of Lochaber who did an excellent job of imparting his enthusiasm for and extensive knowledge of hill navigation, amply illustrated by examples of successful (and unsuccessful!) navigation in some of the Scottish long classics, the claggy 2009 Arrochar Alps being a particular favourite!

Based on the success of this first course, SHR hope to organise future courses in other areas depending on demand, with the next scheduled either for this autumn or next spring, so could anyone who has not yet expressed an interest and is keen to take part please get in touch (e.g., by e-mailing scottishhillrunnersATyahoo.co.uk).

\[The participants were: Edmund Rooney (Lauder); Tony Stapley (Portobello); Carina Convery (Ochils); Isla Campbell (Central); Gayle Tait (Motherwell); Fiona Kelsall (Ochils); Hugh McPherson (U/A); James Purves (Gala); Alan Macaulay (Carnegie); Kirsty Loudon (Penicuik); Fiona Morris (Gala); Jim Winning (Beacon); Sheila Macnab (Gala); David Hunter (Gala); Shelagh King (Gala); and Tosh Brannan (Lothian).\]
