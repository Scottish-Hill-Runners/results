The 2007 Bog and Burn Championship got off to a splendid start with over 150 runners at Wednesday night's Whangie Whizz.

After the initial charge up the hill to reach the stile it was Jethro Lennox (Shettleston), fresh from warm weather training and big game chasing in South Africa, who took the lead and gradually pulled away to finish in 28.13, a winning margin of over two minutes. 2nd down was Tom Smith of Lochaber, closely followed by first vet John Stevenson (Ochil).

But it was the next finisher who once again grabbed the headlines. Yes, only days after a magnificent 7th place and new record at the Anniversary Waltz, it was Angela Mudge (Carnethy), setting yet another new race record. Her time of 30.44 beat her own mark, set last year, by more than 40 seconds.

Second Lady was Emily Strong (Unattached), with third place going to Andrina Buchanan (Central), who was also first vet.

Congratulations to Dave Calder and his Westerlands team for a superbly organised race, including managing the logistics of new car parking facilities at the scout camp down the road, and the fastest prizegiving in history - winners receiving their awards as they crossed the finish line!
