title: "First British Championship Race: Slieve Bearnagh, 4 April"
date: 2009-04-05
excerpt: "Tom Owens, Robbie Simpson, Elke Schmidt, Fiona Maxwell and Dave Scott catch the eye in the first round of the British championship....  '"
---

The first of this year's four British Championship races was the short-category Slieve Bearnagh, which in terms of height gain per kilometre, is about as tough as it gets in the UK.

It was a bit breezy but otherwise conditions were reasonable, if indeed this word can ever be applied to a race with such brutal climbs and descents. Reigning British champion Rob Hope got his 2009 campaign off to the best possible start with a win and new course record, but he was pushed hard by young bro Danny all the way to the line, with Morgan Donnelly in third. Pippa Jackson of Keswick was an impressive ladies winner, finishing almost a minute clear of Elke Schmidt (Bellahouston). Amongst the men first Scot was Tom Owens (ninth overall), followed by Robbie Simpson (fifteenth overall and first junior), who had a great race to mix it with such a strong senior field. The Carnethy duo of Stewart Whitlie and Steven Fallon finished two seconds apart as third and fourth M45, whilst Dave Scott (Ochil) had a very good run to finish third M50. Shettleston's Fiona Maxwell was 1st FV40 and 5th lady.
