title: "Ben Rinnes Hill Race Results And Report"
date: 2019-07-30
excerpt: "Wins for Rob Sinclair (HHR) and Jill Stephen (HBT) at Ben Rinnes on Saturday 27th July"
---

Following the Scottish championship status of last year and with a British championship race at Newtonmore in a weeks time I was expecting a quiet race this year but how wrong I was.The 102 starters was the most we’ve ever had in a non championship year and some of the names on the entry list had my mouth watering at the prospect of a high quality race,and so it proved.Despite overnight rain and light cloud cover things became deceivingly hot with a warm breeze and the field soon became increasingly spread out.The “big four” at the front broke away with the whippet-like running machine that is Rob Sinclair breaking from his leash on the ascent of Ben Rinnes to reach the summit with a couple of minutes advantage.This increased ever more on the return for him to win in an outstanding time for the conditions.I would probably place it number two on the impressive performance list only bettered by Robbie Simpson in 2011.Tom Owens returned six minutes later followed by the fast finishing Kyle Greig who had overhauled last years course record setter Eoin Lennon with him wilting in the heat.Not to be outdone by Rob,Dufftown girl Jill Stephen returned home to win for the third year in a row,again in an impressive time for the conditions and could easily have threatened her own course record had it been cooler.Catriona Morrison had to settle for a distant second with local running guide Jenny Gillies snatching third after emerging from a close pack of three.It was good to see two over 70s complete the course to make full use of the extensive prize list supported by our new sponsor “Eight Lands”,a new gin distillery built in the glen below the summit cone of Ben Rinnes.

At the race start check, attention was drawn to the increasingly ridiculous practice of runners folding their numbers down to the point of not being much bigger than a postage stamp and sticking them onto odd parts of their body.Please spare a thought for checkpoint officials,timekeepers in a busy highland games environment and the compere who may wish to announce your arrival into the arena.There may also be sponsors logos printed on them ie the people who contribute to your prizes.

Two likely lads who should have known better decided to go off-piste on the lower slopes of Ben Rinnes which resulted in their removal from the finish results which was unfortunate for one as he would have otherwise won his age category and also unfortunate for the race as we already had a 10% D.N.F. rate due to the taxing conditions.

Thanks to all the race volunteers and off-road vehicle radio group COTAG.I have, left over, a Highland hillrunners vest,a Metro Aberdeen T-shirt and still from last year a whole running kit of a bladder backpack,waterproofs and compass.Enquire to 01340 871370

Graeme Bartlett (Race Organiser)
