_From organiser Paul Morris:_

The race went smoothly this year with near ideal running conditions - gentle rain cooling the runners on the major climb eased off later on. Damon Rodwell and John Wilkinson battled it out for first place - the result being made more interesting by John's appearing to take a faster line off the second summit. Despite this, Damon held on to his lead to take the honours by 11 seconds. Club membership secretaries note he is currently "unattached", and rumoured to be "available".

In the women's race, although first (Mhairi Mackenzie) and second (Helen Bridle) were seperated by nine places, the winning margin was less than a minute.

The race organisers' suggestion to stick to the paths was treated with the kind of respect only hill runners can muster, as competitors took a greater variety of lines off the second summit than might have been considered possible. Most importantly there was no repeat of last years' accident.

Moorfoot showed they are becoming a force to be reckoned with in hill running circles as they took the mens' team prize, whilst Gala took the ladies team honours.
