Thirty eight runners topped the cairn for this year’s Nebit Downfall, with Andrew Gilmore (Carnethy) picking up where he left off in last year’s Get a Grip Downhill Series. Andrew won the final race of the 2013 series at Caerketton, and last night led the way down The Nebit above Alva, covering the 1.6km course (410m descent) in 5m02s, winning by 6 seconds ahead of first vet 40 Gary Fraser (Ochils). Last year’s series winner, and Nebit record holder, Iain Gilmore (Carnethy) made up the podium in 3rd. Fiona Kelsall (Ochils) made a storming start to the series, finishing first female (and female vet 40) in a new course record, ahead of Deborah Stokoe (Westerlands) and Lisa Blackley (Unnattached). The male veteran categories were hard fought with Steven Fallon (Carnethy) heading home the vet 50s and clubmate Bob Waterhouse the vet 60s. Bob’s partner Cali Ingham (Carnethy) made it a double vet 60 win. Last but very much not least was vet 70 Ian McMannus (Irvine), showing that you’re never too old to embrace the madness of downhill racing!
