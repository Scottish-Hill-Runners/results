Durisdeer entries are now open online via Sientries and will remain so till the 6th June. Due to the race being a British/Scottish championships counter, entries will be online only with no provision for entries on the day. There is no upper limit.

The race usually attracts around 30 competitors and is well known for its friendly atmosphere and possibly the best post-race tea and cakes in the sport. Durisdeer is well located to explore The Lowthers or Galloway Hills further for those looking to make a weekend of it. Durisdeer is a tiny hamlet nestled into the Lowther Hills with around a dozen houses and a small parish church.

Registration will be upstairs in the church but please PARK FIRST in field 200m beyond church.

The route has been described as generally runnable with some steep descents and climbs and with a bit of everything.

Durisdeer is tiny with one road in and out which must be kept clear. This years event is likely to be busy, please park as directed prior to anything else.

Competitors should be able to navigate with map and compass which should be carried along with full waterproof cover (Mandatory)
