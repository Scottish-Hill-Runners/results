_From race organiser Adam Anderson:_

With 50 minutes to go to the start, marshals, mountain rescue and the tea-tent crew outnumbered runners by about 5 to 1 but a late rush saw 34 runners start. Running conditions were near perfect with good visibility and a couple of cooling showers of variable intensity depending on what part of the course you were on.

Alasdair Anthony took the lead on the first climb and was followed closely by Alan Smith. I was able to watch the progress of the lead two from a safe distance with Alan staying in touch till the second road crossing before Alasdair stretched to a comfortable winning lead.

Nicola Davies won the ladies' race from Shirley Singh with Tilly Smith in third.

The organiser apologises for times only recorded to the minute and not the second - he never made this clear to the volunteer timekeeper.

After the race, runners, marshals and mountain rescue were able to enjoy the sunshine and endless amounts of tea and cakes. Thanks to all our helpers and mountain rescue as always.
