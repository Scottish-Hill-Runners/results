Some 50 young athletes gathered at Aberfoyle on Sunday 1 September for the Scottish Trial races for the Hill Running Junior Home International, to be held in 3 weeks over the same courses. In addition to the U16 and U19 Trial races, Sunday's races included U14 categories. This year's courses were new, with the Trial having taken place for the last 4 years at Menstrie, near Stirling. Race organiser, Hugh Buchanan explained "When we host the Junior Home International, we provide accommodation for the teams. There is outdoor centre accommodation available adjacent to these courses, which made this venue ideal. It made sense to bring the Trial races here too, so our athletes get the benefit of racing the courses in advance, and our selectors get the benefit of seeing results from the courses to be raced."  
In the U14, raced over 3.8km with 225m of climb, Laura Stark (Kilbarchan) led the girls home. In the boys race, Joshua Boyle from Hyndburn in Lancashire took the honours, with Tristan Rees (Fife) leading the Scots home. The U16 girls also raced over 3.8km with 225m of climb, with Jennifer Ricketts (Deeside) the leading finisher. The U16 boys and U19 girls had a longer course of 4.9km and 275m climb, which turned at the hilltop with great views over the plain of the upper Forth Valley. Logan Rees (Fife) led the boys home, and Louise Mercer (Gala) victorious in the girls' race. The U19 boys had a looping course which combined elements of the 2 shorter races, amounting to 7.0km and 375m climb: Ross Campbell (Edinburgh University) led home strongly, despite having only taken up hill running this season.

Hugh Buchanan
