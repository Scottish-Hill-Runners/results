The elusive Red Moss hill race .. is still on this year..but the route has changed after consultation with the local landowners to avoid the sensitive heather moorland on Hare hill - which has nesting birds and where the ground is soft and gets easily cut-up.

The new route is longer at 10.5km (old route was 9km) and now goes over West and East Kip instead. (there may be late changes to the proposed route as I get advice from the relevant landowners) The race will start from Red Moss car park, go along the road as usual , then take the usual path towards the drove road (passing the turn-off to Hare Hill and instead continuing to the Kips), up West Kip , over East Kip , then turn sharp left at the col to follow the path back towards the Howe, then from the bottom of the glen turn left (north-east) to follow the foot-path through Green Cleuch, and return by the path, to the road at Bavelaw Castle and back to the Red moss car park. (the race won’t go round Threipmuir reservoir this year - some will be delighted as it used to be a fast finish on the flat! not popular with hillrunners!) The new route will still be fast running on paths - with a guestimated winning time of 40 mins. Reminder that there is limited car parking at Red Moss car park, please car share or use public transport , pre-entry via sientries with race numbers limited to 100. \[but there is still Free race entry on the day for anyone who cycles to the start! Ok this is blatant favouritism to cyclists , but it is the Edinburgh Festival of Cycling http://www.edfoc.org.uk/ and cycling to the start is the best way to warm-up!\]
