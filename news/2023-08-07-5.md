Race Report

There was a sense of relief from the organisers and the 20 marshals from Braemar Mountain Rescue Team when the worst of the low cloud lifted for the first wave of 21 runners who set off at 10am. The main wave of a further 48 runners set off an hour later. The weather stayed dry until mid afternoon when a slow moving deluge reminded competitors of the importance of carrying the mandatory kit!

The race takes in the 9 Munro's bordering Glenshee ski area and much of the route is above the 900 metre contour line, some of the runners reported that the rain fell as sleet on the highest summits. That's August for you in the Scottish Highlands!

The early start option meant that all bar two of the runners met the 2.30pm cut off for the A93 road crossing before the final leg of three Munro's. 63 of the 69 starters completed the full route.

Results wise Jonathan Turnbull was 1st overall and 1st MV40 in 3:51:17 followed by Lewis Taylor and Ryan Gordon (also 2nd MV40).

First female was Ella Peters 4:17:34 with Clara-Jane Irvine in second and Nicky Spinks 3rd female (and 1st FV50).

Stephen Winter was 3rd MV40. Walter Henderson was first MV50 with local Deeside Runners Donald Kerridge and Alan Sealy claiming the other two positions. Gordon Price first MV60 followed by Ian Hamilton and Sandy Dunn.

First FV40 was Rachel Little followed by Eleanor Smith and Nicola Adams-Hendry. The second FV50 was Corri Black with Elspeth Kirkparick in third spot.

Well done to all the competitors, it was good to see so many smiling faces crossing the finishing line.

Special thanks to Braemar Mountain Rescue for providing safety cover, Glenshee Ski Centre for hosting the event and providing welcome refreshments, High Terrain Events and Protay Timing for professional race services.
