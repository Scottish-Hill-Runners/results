_From race organiser Roy Morrison:_

The conditions for running were pretty good, firm and dry underfoot, a fresh breeze at low level but a bit more windy at the top - for those that enjoy the view before returning.

The entries were down a bit this year but Alan Cherry’s time at 16.28 was just 17 seconds short of the 2008 record, whereas Toni McIntosh at 21.55 was 1min 20 sec behind last year's women's record. William Limond, a 13 year old “unattached” from Kirkmichael posted a praise worthy 21.32, a lad who has potential.

We are expecting the Race to be run again next year to help our Funday to go with a swing on 20 August 2011 - so note it in the diary and bring your friends.

For those interested in community issues, our fundraiser to help keep the Straiton toilets open for our visitors raised £267 – thanks to all including runners! Come back again any time, the loos will be open!
