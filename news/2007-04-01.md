Preliminary results of yesterday’s Donard Commedagh, the first British Championship race of the year, are [here](http://www.nimra.org.uk/news.asp?nid=322). The 1-2-3 (out of 240) were Rob Hope (Pudsey and Bramley), Rob Jebb (Bingley) and Danny Hope (Pudsey and Bramley).

Alasdair Anthony (Ochils) was the first Scot, in eighth place, sandwiched between Tim Davies (Mercia) and Jim Davies (Borrowdale), the leading M40. The HBT duo of Ray Ward and Jamie Thin were 27th and 35th with Angela Mudge (Carnethy) close behind in 39th, first woman and over four minutes ahead of defending British champion Natalie White (Bingley). Jill Mykura (Carnethy) was sixth, and Carnethy were second Ladies team behind Bingley.

The men’s and women’s winning times were both records, Angela Mudge’s 1:11:28 being well over a minute faster than her 1999 effort.

And the prizegiving wasnt over for Carnethy as they took home a crate of wine for having the most runners from one club at the event!!
