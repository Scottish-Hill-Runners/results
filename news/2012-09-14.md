The Ben did its best to thwart those making their bid for the summit of Britain’s highest mountain, with high winds, rain and clag above the Red Burn. Lochaber runner Finlay Wild won for the third year in a row, the first Scot ever to do so.

Previous multiple winner Rob Jebb of Bingley Harriers was first to the summit by some margin, but Wild caught him to finish around one minute ahead in a time of just under 1 hour 30 minutes. First lady was Sharon Taylor also of Bingley, closely followed with a great run by Eilidh Wardlaw of HBT.

Lochaber Mountain Rescue report that they were kept busy dealing with 4 incidents in the race at the same time. 3 casualties suffered from hypothermia and one from a shoulder injury, the latter having to be stretchered off by RAF rescue helicopter. All were reported to have been discharged subsequently from the Belford Hospital.....
