_From Matt Davis_

With near ideal conditions on the day a field of just over 50 turned out to support the return of this classic race and whilst it might not meet the long race criteria it certainly packs a lot in and gives a real roller coaster of an outing. The partially marked course ensured the mix of race hardened veterans to first timers tackling the course could find their way round with relative ease and maximum enjoyment. On the day Carnethy took the honours; Andy Fallas & Stuart Whitlie in keeping with the nature and spirit of the event declared a truce and crossed the line as joint winners and for the ladies Helen Bonsor finished strongly on the final descent to arrive home first. With plenty smiles & many a tired pair of legs, the runners were handed their finishing goodies before heading off to the village hall to recuperate on soup and cakes.

Thanks to all the back room staff, marshals, sweepers, local land owners, sponsors and runners for helping to make the race a great success; with just over £700 being raised for "Save The Crook" campaign.

Fingers crossed for its return in 2013.
