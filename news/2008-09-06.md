This year's Ben Nevis Race was part of the [Buff Skyrunner World Series](http://www.buffskyrunner.com/results/).

Agusti Roc (FSA) just beat Rob Jebb (Bingley), whilst Angela Mudge (Carnethy) was first woman. Conditions were near perfect - very dry, cool and a bit windy on top. There were 448 starters.

[Results](http://www.fsa-sky.org/new/images/results/ben%20nevis%20race%202008%20-%20result.pdf)|[Photos](http://www.flickr.com/photos/scottishhillrunners/sets/72157607237853295/)

Angela Mudge (Carnethy) then finished 2nd overall in the Buff Skyrunner World Series, after taking 3rd place in the final race, the gruelling Sentiero Delle Grigne in Italy... This report from Jethro Lennox:

"A group of us went across to Italy to compete in the final of the Buff Skyrunner World Series, Sunday, September 21, at the Sentiero Delle Grigne race in northwestern Italy. 418 runners from 13 nations lined up for this extremely tough and technical race up and down the steep Grigne mountains near Lecco. The 43 km long course had a total climb of 6,400 metres.

Highlight was Angela Mudge (Carnethy) finishing 3rd in a time of 5 hours 50 minutes and taking 2nd overall in this years Buff World Series. Other Scottish runners competing were Tom Owens (Shettleston) who finished an excellent 8th overall in 5 hours 9 minutes, with Andrew Symonds (Carnegie) in 11th in 5 hours 14 minutes closely followed 8 seconds later by Jethro Lennox (Shettleston). Symonds also managed to complete the Buff World Series, finishing 3rd overall.
