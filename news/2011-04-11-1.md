It was a Shettleston and Scotland 1-2 at the first British Championship race of the 2011 season on Saturday. The tough 12.5mile long category Mourne Peaks race was won in emphatic style by Tom Owens, who pulled away from the chasing pack on the long final descent from the high point of the course, Slieve Commedagh. He was chased hard by team-mate Jethro Lennox who in turn pulled away from third placed Rob Jebb of Bingley and one time leader Graham Pearce (Pudsey). Kenny Richmond (Bellahouston) had a strong run to finish 13th and 6th Vet.

Matt Sullivan (Shettleston) was unable to repeat his fine performance at Birnam where he finished third and split his teammates, but neverthless had a good run to finish 40th in a top quality field. David Dickson of Westerlands ran well for 84th and 8th V50. Carnethy teammates Willie Gibson and Joanne Anderson were the remaining Scottish runners, having their own close battle which ended with Willie in 109th and Joanne in 112th.
