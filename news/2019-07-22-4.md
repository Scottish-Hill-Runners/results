Starting with the Lee Pen Hill Race which includes 1,200 brutal feet of climb and then foot burning descent within the ‘modest’ 3 mile course. The race was won by Alasdair McLeod of Shettleston Harriers in the very quick time of 25:23 in hot and muggy conditions with the morning rain burning off to make the steep, off camber descent particularly slippy and challenging. In a smaller field of 16 runners compared to recent years (30 ran in both 2017 & 18) Alasdair McLeod opened a large gap on the climb and was too far ahead to be troubled by expert descender Brian Marshall (1st Vet) on the return. 3rd place went to Marty Davidson of the Moorfoot Runners club based in the Tweed valley, with team mate Colin Williams finishing 5th to collect the prize for 1st Innerleithen local.

First female home was Sarah Lilleman of Dark Peak Fell Runners with a very impressive 33:16 (7th overall), not far ahead of the home town’s Caroline Wallace in 8th overall, 2nd woman and 1st Vet (35:35) with Carol Fortune of Gala Harriers making up the top 3 and 2 nd Vet.

Many thanks to Walkerburn resident Bob Johnson, for sponsoring the Hill Race – there is a substantial prize fund for the various placings with the winner collecting £80 for first prize.
