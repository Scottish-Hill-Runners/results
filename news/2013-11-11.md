_From Race Organiser Cameron Scott_

Shettleston's Matt Sullivan won this years' Run And Become Tinto Hill Race after pulling away from host club's Andy Fallas on the descent. Third spot was taken by Carnethy veteran Paul Faulkner. Carnethy also supplied the first lady home in Charlotte Morgan.

231 runners started the race, and there would have been a few more if they had read the race instructions properly and brought the required mandatory kit. Anyone who heard Keith Burns at the SHR AGM, held after the race, will be in no doubt as to why race safety is paramount.

I'd like to thank all the runners who took part and seemed to enjoy the event, the helpers and marshals from Carnethy and Tinto Hill Runners and Adrian at Run And Become for continued support.
