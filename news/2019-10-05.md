Earlier this year a "skyline" round of L Lomond was finally completed by Luke Arnott. The full report appeared on the Westies website, and a copy of the document is now in the SHR database archive: [Round of Loch Lomond](https://www.scottishhillrunners.uk/Download.aspx?item=Round%20\(Luke%20Arnott\).docx).

This item will appear in the Long Distance Records section of the SHR website.

Luke's summary of the route and timings appears below:

In summary the round is a circumnavigation of Loch Lomond going over all the highest hills closest to the Loch plus some extras i.e. a skyline of sorts, starting and finishing in Balmaha going anticlockwise. It's therefore the East side of the Loch followed by the Arrochar Alps Munros and The Cobbler then most of the Luss Hills and a final road section including Duncryne. The splits from the supported round are below. I did the round twice. The first time solo 22/23 April 2019 with food drops in 38hrs 47 mins and the second supported 19/20 May 2019 in 34hrs 24 mins. It's approximately 75 miles and 30000ft ascent (although that could be slightly conservative as I've gone for the lowest numbers, calculated by different methods). The times are relatively slow for such distances but there is a fair bit of rough, pathless ground. The summit splits are below. I have a GPX file from a GPS device carried. I'm not aware of the round having been done previously. A more detailed account is here:?https://westerlandsccc.co.uk/hill-round-of-loch-lomond/ The account is perhaps a bit long but I'd been trying and failing on the round over about 4 years which is all explained. The successful solo round was my 15th attempt.
