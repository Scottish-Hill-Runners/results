Mark Harris reports from his Norman's Law and Emily Hill races 8th April 2012

_Local club runners from Fife AC and Dundee Hawkhill take the honours at Norman's Law._

_Chris Russell (Fife AC) triumphed in good running conditions in 32.26, and for the second year running won the Alan Milligan quaich. Second was Ryan Stewart (Dundee Hawkhill) in 33.46 and not far behind was John Hammond (Carnethy, 33.54). The women's race was won by another Fife AC runner, Alison McGill (38.56) and followed by Rhona van Rensburg (Fife AC, 39.17) and Julia Connor (Moorfoot, 39.53)._

_First male over 40 was in fact over 50 SHR hill champion, Alan Smith (Deeside), in fifth position overall, and fresh from Clachnaben the day before. Craig Love (Dundee Hawkhill) took the over 40 honours, and Tony Martin (Fife AC) won the over 60 category. Susan Harley took the women's veteran category and Jane Chalmers the over 50._

_The junior champions were Connor McDougall in 42.12 and Mhaire Roberts in 52.45 (both unattached)._

_In the Emily Hill race a good turnout saw Sol Sweeney from Strathern Harriers win and fellow club runner Catriona Roberts take the girls title._

_Many thanks to the marshalls, tea makers, and other volunteers, Run and Become, and especially my co-organiser, John Kay._

_MH_
