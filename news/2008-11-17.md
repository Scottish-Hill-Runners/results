From race organiser Roger Boswell:

The Druim Fada Chase was the second race in this year's Lochaber Winter League. Sandy Hastings invented the League, and in 1989 finished eleventh and last in the Druim Fada Chase. Nineteen years on Sandy looked down from a clear blue sky and must have been chuffed to watch 71 runners take part, including a large number of foreigners, especially Highland Hill Runners.

Local Ruari Watt took a firm hold on this year's race and ran out a comfortable winner in 45:00. Not far behind Colin Donnelly lost his grip on second place as Craig Mitchell and Ross Bannerman (Highland) sprinted past him. Forty-nine year old Colin did win the over 40s veterans' race.

In the women's race, Roxy Bannerman of Highland Hill Runners took the spoils from a galaxy of local talent headed by Amanda Blackhall with Jo Schreiber, Hannah Barnes and Nix Forster close behind.

The next race in the Lochaber Winter League is the one Sandy Hastings used to organise, the very popular Canal Bank 4-mile race, Sunday 23 November, 11.00 am at the Moorings Hotel Car Park Banavie. Will Sandy see the first ever 100+ field?
