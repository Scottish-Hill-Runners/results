---
title: "Mudge Masters the World in Poland, 28 August"
date: 2010-08-30
excerpt: "Angela Mudge has won the W40 class at the World Masters in Poland, with four other Scots making the top ten in their age group............."
---

A decade on from her victory in the World Trophy, Angela Mudge won the F40 class in the 10th WMRA/WMA Master World Mountain Running Championships, which were held in Korbielow, Poland. Battling the rain throughout the 8km course, which climbed 960m to the finish on the summit of Pilsko mountain, Angela triumphed in emphatic style, coming home almost four minutes ahead of her nearest rival. There's an official report [here](http://www.wmra.ch/index.php?option=com_content&task=view&id=689&Itemid=2), and as yet unofficial results are [here](http://www.wmmrc2010.org/en/results.html)

There were impressive top ten finishes for Jane Kerridge and Anne Stone (5th and 7th in W55), Ann Anderson (6th in W65) and Keith Burns (8th in M65).

The other Scots included: Pauline Larmour (13th in W45), Elaine Stewart (15th in W50), John Stevenson (28th in M45), Colin Larmour (38th in M45), Martin Laing (17th in M50), Malcolm Patterson (30th in M50), John Blair-Fish (24th in M55) and Martin Hulme (22nd in M60).

For most of the competitors the real ordeal began after their race was over, when amid chaotic scenes, the wet, cold and tired runners struggled back down the mountain for a kilometre only to find that their dry kit was strewn across the floor of the building in disorganised heaps. Thankfully everyone eventually came out unharmed and once the small matter of a further 6km or so of descent on foot was dealt with, the Scots regrouped to enjoy the medal ceremony and final night's entertainment........
