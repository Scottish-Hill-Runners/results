The UKA Inter Counties Fell & Hill Running Championship comes to Scotland for the first time on Saturday 28 May, where competitors will take on the challenge of the Broughton Heights Hill Race. The race is 10km with 700m of climb and race details can be found [here]( http://www.scottishhillracing.co.uk/RaceDetails.aspx?RaceID=RA-0266)

Scotland is split into four districts for hill running competition, Central (comprising Central, Fife & Tayside), East (Lothian & Borders), North (Grampian & Highlands) and West (Strathclyde & Dumfries & Galloway).

Selection for each district will be based mainly on form in hill races up to the selection date, Monday 9 May (nb: different to the date (3 May) published in the International Handbook).

If you have any queries about the event or selection (suitable races in which to show form etc.) then please contact your district hill running co-ordinator:

The Broughton Heights Hill Race which is hosting the Inter Counties event is open for entries on the day. It is also the first counter in the 2011 UKA McCain Mountain Running Challenge, see details below:

The three race McCain Mountain Running Challenge comprises the following events:

Race 1 : Broughton Heights Hill Race, 28 May

Race 2 : European Championship Trial Race (uphill only), Keswick, 12 June

Race 3 : World Championship Trial Race, Witton Park Blackburn, 13 August

Each race will award individual cash prizes as follows (for both male and female categories): 1st : £250, 2nd : £125, 3rd : £100, 4th : £75, 5th : £50, 6th : £40, 7th : £25. There will also be prizes for the overall series, with the best 2 out of the 3 races to count.

Only UK athletes, as defined in the Challenge rules, are eligible for race and series prizes. Further details on the Challenge and the rules can be found [here]( http://www.uka.org.uk/competitions/mccain-mountain-running-challenge)
