_From race organiser John Coyle:_

There was a fair cover of mist on the tops and gusts at the loch which meant that Steven's, Stewart's, and John's efforts at erecting the marquee the night before were on test. The marquee survived … just … and everyone had the grace to stand inside it before the start so that the hard work was worth it.

The mist cleared and the sun even managed to shine so that the Ben More ridge was in full glory yet the wind continued to blow. With a race like the Monserette it’s great to have everyone safely home and even better when everyone has had a brilliant time. Thanks to everyone for coming along and to the marshals for keeping everything in check. A big well done to Sara, Peter, and a swollen ankle for supporting each other all the way (although she left him on the Ben descent since ‘I’ll be fine on the road’).
