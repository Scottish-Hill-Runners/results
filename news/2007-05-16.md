title: "Durris Mast Hill Race gets Cosmics' Summer Series under way, 15 May"
date: 2007-05-16
excerpt: "Craig Mitchell (Lochaber) and Anita Hamilton (Cosmics) won the inaugural Durris Mast Hill Race, the first of the four in Cosmics' Summer Series.  '"
---

This is a new race, and the first counter in Cosmics' Summer Hill Race Series. The distance is 8.8km (5.5mi) and the ascent 305m (1000ft). According to [Wikipedia](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/List_of_tallest_buildings_and_structures_in_Great_Britain) the Mast itself is the equal-highest structure in Scotland at 306.6m (1006ft).

Race organiser Fiona Dahl reports:

"Lochaber runner Craig Mitchell took first place in the Durris Mast Race last night, closely followed by ex-Lochaber runner (now Cosmic) Jason Williamson. Dan Gooch from Deeside took third place while a tremendous run from Simon Pearce gave him fourth place. Carl Pryce (Cosmics) was first M40 and Peter Jennings (Metro) was first M50. Anita Hamilton (Cosmics) was first female.

Thankfully the weather stayed dry although conditions underfoot were pretty wet. The ‘interesting’ route including a very dark section through thick forest was enjoyed by most (except maybe the guy with shades on!). The next race in the Cosmic Hill Race Series will be the Balmedie Beach Bash on Tues 19 June - more details on the [Cosmics' web site](http://www.cosmics.org.uk)."
