For any trail runners out there, especially those of you looking at the newly-created Scottish National Trail, it looks like Deeside Runners have set the first record...and laid down a challenge:

Deeside Runners have just run a relay along the Scottish National Trail from Kirk Yetholm to Cape Wrath. It is 800km, almost all off road and definitely counts as hill running for most of the way. It took us 90 hours and 20 minutes, starting at midnight on Thursday the 23rd May and finishing at 18.20 on Monday the 27th. There were 35 of us in total, we divided it into 82 legs and had two runners on almost all of them. Some of us ran 100km in total, one ran only 4km. There's more here: http://thebigrelay.blogspot.co.uk/

We're pretty sure this is a record, because no-one has tried it before. The challenge is now there for any other hill running club that wants to give it a go.

Dave Hirst
