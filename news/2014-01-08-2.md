Thrashing through ten kilometres of mud, peat bog and heather is not everybody’s idea of Boxing Day fun, yet forty-plus athletes enjoyed the best weather of the festive season so far to complete Lochaber Athletic Club’s annual Boxing Day race to the summit of Cruim Leacainn, just north of Fort William. Despite very wet conditions under foot, Shettleston runner Alistair MacLeod completed the course in a very fast 42:57. Lochaber Athletic Club’s David Rogers had posted a time of 41:36 back in 2006, but the course has been lengthened by 500 to 600m since then, so Alistair has created a new record rather than breaking the old one. Lochaber athletes Tom Smith and Nick Sedgwick completed the leading trio in this year’s race, with visiting Westerlands runner, Paula Gardiner, the first lady home in 1:02:10 – a few seconds ahead of Isla MacKay and Fiona Thompson, both LAC. Jim Treas urer 1:07:25, Mick Tighe 1:10:31 and Jimmy Stewart 1:10:40 kept the flag flying for the ‘old men’ in what was a most enjoyable run. The race has become a wee bit of a social event in the past few years, for runners and supporters alike, helped considerably by Kathy Tighe and her merry band of ‘volunteers’, providing scrummy bites before, during and after the race. In Lochaber thoughts now turn to the Aonach Mor Uphill race on January 1st – Happy New Year meanwhile.
