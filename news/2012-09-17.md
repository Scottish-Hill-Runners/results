Report from Angela Mudge:

Jungfrau Marathon – World Long Distance Mountain Running Challenge

September 9th and 10th

This year’s World Longs was held on one of the most famous mountain races in the Alps – the Jungfrau Marathon. A 42.2km race starting in Interlaken with 10km on flat roads, then heading up the valley to Lauterbrunnen where after 26km and only 250m of climb, the race really begins. The final 16km climbs 1600m running through Wengen and finishing with stunning views of the Eiger at 2100m, Kleine Scheidegg.

The women’s race was held on the Saturday with Angela Mudge leading the Scots home in 7th place and the team finishing in 6th place overall. Sunday saw an exciting men’s race with the Scots packing closely on the flatter sections and then coming through the field on the climb. Robbie Simpson led the Brits (and Scots) home to finish an excellent 9th place overall. The men were 4th team – a brilliant result in this strongly contested uphill only event.
