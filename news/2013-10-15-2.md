The second race of the Killin double header weekend and again the weather gods were incredibly kind. Cool, bright sunshine, and not a cloud in the sky. The field was depleted to just under 30 hardy souls, most of whom had done the Tarmachan race the day before. Alan Smith of Deeside had intended to head home after Saturday's race but the opportunity to revenge his defeat by Manny Gorman of Westerlands was just too good to miss and he was back and this time it was serious. So game on ......Alan went off like a man demented, climbing really quickly on the short sharp ascent of Sron a Clachain, and it looked like curtains for Manny, when Alan beat him to the summit. But it was not to be, although Alan's descending skills are legendary, Manny did the business and somehow managed to get past Alan on the way down to complete his second victory of the weekend in 23:18 with Alan just over 30 seconds behind. First lady was Julia Connor of Tinto in just under 31 minutes. Plenty of soup, sandwiches, and cakes were available back at the nearby hall where the prize-giving took place. Great weekend double-header of races.
