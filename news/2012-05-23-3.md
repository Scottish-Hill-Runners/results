_From Hugh Buchanan_

The World Masters Mountain Running Championships 2012 took place at Buhlertal in the German Black Forest on Saturday 19 May 2012 over a 9.6km uphill-only course with 780m of climb ([http://www.wm.tv-buehlertal.de/](http://www.wm.tv-buehlertal.de/)). The event comprises separate classes for each sex in 5-year age categories: in F45, Sue Ridley (Edinburgh AC) took an excellent bronze medal in a field of 39 with a time of 54.46; in M40, Paul Rogan (Forres) finished 18th among a field of 120 with a time of 48.15; in F55, Elaine Stewart (Cosmics) finished 16th in 70.22; and in M50, Hugh Buchanan (Central) took 61.43 to finish two-thirds down his race in 102nd. There was also an open race for those under 35, and Catriona Buchanan (Ochils) finished 3rd woman in 52.32, just 2 days after racing at the Grand Ballon race in France.

In common with many continental races, the course was run over surfaced and forest roads, with just the last 1km on a mountain path. The combination of the surface, the relatively low climb per km, and no traditional British descent, meant that leg speed and road shoes were advantages. The age-category starts took place at 20 minute intervals, with the M and F75 classes going off first, and the youngest M and F35 class some 3 hours later. That meant that congestion was never a problem, despite there being over 1000 competitors racing over the same course. Although a World Championship, the only qualification for entry was age: at the front, the standard was very high - at the back, life was much more convivial. The participants were predominantly German, but there were also strong contingents from other continental nations such as Italy, France, Poland, Slovenia and Slovakia. The weather was sunny and hot, so the 4 water stations on the course and at the finish were well used.

If you fancy taking part in something similar, the European Masters Championships is in Bludenz in Austria on 1 July 2012 ([http://www.berglaufteam.com/](http://www.berglaufteam.com/)).
