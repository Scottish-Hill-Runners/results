On Saturday 21 June 2014, Paul Giblin shattered his own West Highland Way record, taking 47 minutes off last year's already hugely impressive time to record a startling 14:20:11. He was pushed hard for the first half by newcomer Robbie Britton, whose 14:47:48 makes him the second sub-15 finisher and would have won any other race easily.

The ladies' race was also of fine quality, with Fionna Ross at 18:45:04 and Rosie Bell at 19:02:19 taking third and fourth places on the ladies' all-time list.

Please see Adrian Stott's reports of the [men's](http://westhighlandwayrace.org/2014/06/24/report-of-the-mens-race-by-adrian-stott/) and [ladies'](http://westhighlandwayrace.org/2014/06/25/report-of-the-womens-race-by-adrian-stott/) races as well as full results and further blog links at the [West Highland Way Race site](http://westhighlandwayrace.org/).
