Stewart Whitlie was 1st M40 (and 2nd overall), Dave Scott was 1st M50, and Graham Arthur 1st M60. Jacqui Higginbottom was 1st F40, and Elaine Stewart was 1st F50.  '  '"
---

The inaugural SHR Long Classics Series reached its conclusion at the Pentland Skyline with Al Anthony and Es Tresidder denying Stewart Whitlie any last minute chance of catching Simon Peachey. Simon had established a sufficient lead early in the series to have the luxury of not needing to run Two Breweries or Pentland Skyline, however if Al Anthony had done one more long race, he would likely have pipped Simon in the end with wins at Two Breweries and Pentland Skyline.

In the women's competition Kate Jenkins took the title by the narrowest of margins over Jacqui Higginbottom, moving 2 points in front in the final race.

In terms of age category winners, Stewart Whitlie was 1st M40 in 2nd place overall. Dave Scott was 1st M50, and Graham Arthur was 1st M60. Jacqui Higginbottom was 1st F40 (and 2nd lady), and Elaine Stewart was 1st F50.

50 runners finished at least 4 races to complete the series, and David Riach (placed 4th overall) was the most prolific runner with 8 races out of 9.
