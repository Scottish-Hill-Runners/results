On a lovely day at the Ballater Highland Games, a tiring Dan Whitehead (Deeside Runners) managed to hold off the fast finishing youngster Sasha Chepelin (Cosmics) by one second to win his home hill race with Robert Watson (Musselburgh AC) coming in third. Steph Provan (Deeside Runners) showed that all those Tuesday evening hill sessions at Glentanar are paying off with a comprehensive win in the ladies race, almost 4 minutes up on Kate Kitchin (Lothian) and Lizzie Kirby (London).  
As usual, there was a large turn out on a Thursday afternoon (don't these people work at all!), 65 runners lined up to complete the 3 mile race which heads through the village before the short but steep ascent of Craigcoillich and then returning to the games field.  
The 1978 race record of 16:59 held by Fred Reeves was never under any threat but it was a keenly contested race nonetheless with the evergreen Alan Smith (Deeside) getting the better of Adrian Davis (Carnethy) in the Super Vet category in fourth and fifth respectively.  
Seventeen runners managed to make the 25 minute cut off time to claim their five pound reward.

Andy Bain
