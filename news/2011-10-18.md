_Dave Scott writes:_

There was a good turnout of 12 for the latest SHR navigation course in Fort William, which was based at Alan Kimber’s Calluna bunkhouse in the town. The course was as ever ably delivered by mountain guide and hill runner Bruce Poll, who travelled up from North Wales for the weekend, with assistance from Dave Scott. The course participants were:

Linda MacEwan, Lochaber  
Anita Hamilton and Colin Taylor, Cosmics  
Morgane Artacho, Cairngorm  
Frank McPartland and Paddy McDonald, HELP  
Peter McClelland and Simon Harrison, HBT  
Jean Jumelle, Carnethy  
Luis Molero-Lopez, Lauder Limpers  
Johnston Orr, Westerlands  
Jenny Munro, unattached

As usual, the course was divided between time spent indoors on the theory followed by practical sessions on the hill. Torrential rain much of the time kept things interesting, especially for those with leaking map cases! The weather cleared slightly for the navigation test on Sunday in the Eagle Crag area, with Linda and Luis coming in just ahead of Peter and Simon, both pairs having bagged all 8 checkpoints. The others weren’t far behind, although the number of checkpoints visited varied!

Many thanks to Bruce for instructing on the course, and to all the participants.
