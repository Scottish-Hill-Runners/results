Details from organiser, Rob Reid:

This Relay Race follows the Kintyre Way, Scotland’s newest long-distance path, from Tarbert to Campbeltown, on Saturday 10th May.

It traverses some stunning scenery with views across the sea to Arran, Islay, Jura, Gigha and Bute. There is varied terrain, from boggy hill paths and forestry tracks to beaches and seaside rocks.

It is billed as Scotland’s Most Sociable Ultra-Running Event. Why?

\* Enter as a relay team with friends or colleagues

\* Join an on-the-spot team and make new friends

\* Chat with fellow-runners along the way

\* Bring family and friends to explore this undiscovered area

\* Lots of non-race activities for all ages

\* Enjoy a traditional ceilidh in the evening (music, dancing and food)

3 events in 1 - take your pick!

\* Ultra Relay Run: 66 miles, starting Tarbert 06:00am. You decide how to share out the 66 miles among your team of 2 to 6 runners. Enthusiasts can even do the full 66 miles solo!

\* Novices Relay Run: 35 miles, starting Tayinloan 09:30. Teams of three doing 13 + 8 + 14 miles.

\* Long-Distance Walk: choose 16, 22 or 31 miles.

More details [here](http://www.kintyrewayrelay.co.uk  )
