Jethro Lennox (Shettleston) took the proverbial bull by the horns and dominated the Kilpatricks race from the off, and in good underfoot conditions he posted an excellent time, finishing in 44:31, a mere 8 seconds outside Ian Murphy's 1997 record. A trademark demon descent saw Andy McCall (Greenock) move up (or should that be down?) into a fine 2nd place, ahead of Bob Wiseman (Ochil) and Dougal Ross (Bellahouston). Ronnie Gallagher (Carnethy) was first V40 in 6th.

Despite a "wee detour" on the descent Elke Schmidt held on to her first place in the ladies race, to finish in 52:32, but was disappointed not to get closer to the record. She had the consolation of leading Bellahouston to the ladies team award, as did Jethro with Shettleston in the mens. Iona Robertson (Bellahouston) was 2nd lady, followed by Val Houston of host club Westerlands, who was also 1st V40.
