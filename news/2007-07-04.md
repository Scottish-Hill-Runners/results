---
title: "Marshall triumphs through red mud and over Black Hill, 3 July"
date: 2007-07-04
excerpt: "Earlston's Civic Week includes the (junior) White and (senior) Black Hill Races.  '"
---

[Lauderdale Limpers](http://www.borderevents.com/sport/6.html) have revived the White and Black Hill Races as part of Earlston's Civic Week. They were rewarded with a good turnout of juniors for the White Hill Race and forty seniors for the Black Hill.

We're told the Black Hill course has been lengthened a bit. It's a mixture of cross-country and hill: through the fields and red, red mud to the foot of the hill, up its steep grassy flank to the fine view from the trig point, down the hill's spine, round the back and then home through the fields plus red mud again.

Brian Marshall (HELP) won comfortably from Euan Jardine (Gala H), who hadn't fully recovered from a viral infection and wasn't able to sustain his challenge. Moorfoots' Michael McGovern and Darin Dougal were third and fourth, with Rachel Fagan (Gala H) the first woman home in seventeenth place overall.
