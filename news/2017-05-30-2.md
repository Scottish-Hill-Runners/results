---
title: "Isle of Jura, 2017 Results"
date: 2017-05-30
excerpt: "A new course record for Finlay Wild (Lochaber) and a win for Jill Stephen (HBT) ..."
---

All 6 Haineses ran the race, including Hector, the 2014 record breaker. He could not keep up with Finlay Wild who took another 1 min 16 sec off Hector's best time so far, and confirmed suspicions. Jasmin Paris withdrew a few minutes before the start of the race, with a smile on her face, and the top lady on the day was Jill Stephen of HBT.

22 men achieved sub-4 hour whisky tumblers, and 4 ladies took home a sub 4:45 whisky tumbler. (They are all inscribed "completed in under 4 hours").

Note that 6 of the first 10 and 12 of the first 20 finishers were from Scottish clubs.

Mark Rigby's record of 3:06:59 stood for 20 years. Hector's of 3:06:30 for another 3 years, and Finlay has whittled it down to 3:05:14. Why do marathons and the Cuillin Ridge come to mind?
