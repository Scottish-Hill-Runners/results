On 11th March in Glasgow and 25th March in Edinburgh. Its your chance to hear Angela Mudge, one of the world’s greatest hill runners, talking about her trip to the [Everest Marathon](http://www.everestmarathon.org.uk/index.html) last November, which culminated in her smashing the women’s record for this unique race. Hear how she prepared for the event, and how she had to cope with the long walk in to the start of what is the world’s highest race, at Gorak Shep, close to Everest Base Camp, at 5200m. As if this wasn’t challenge enough – think of the event as “a 20 day endurance event, culminating in a marathon” - let her talk you through the race itself, with its 2000m of descent (plus a mere 500m or so of climb) and temperatures veering from -20 to +20°C.

Funds raised from Angela’s talk will be donated to the [St. Marks Hospital Foundation Mosaic Appeal](http://www.mosaicappeal.org)

The talk costs £5, and the Glasgow talk will take place at the Tiso Outdoor Experience, Couper Street, Glasgow at 7pm on March 11th. Tickets can be obtained from Run 4 It shops, or call 0141 221 4300, or email glasgowcity@run-4-it.com The Edinburgh talk will take place on 25th March at Run-4-It, Lothian Road. Tickets again £5.
