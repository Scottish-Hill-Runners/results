51 competitors turned out for this ‘uphill only’ race. With heavy rain the previous day, the streams were full and made the three crossings a bit hairy and took some of the runners by surprise. There were lots of newcomers to the race, as it was also the trial for the forthcoming UK senior home internationals in Keswick on 25th May.  
Robbie Simpson took off from the start was the clear winner in 36.17. Hector Haines (HBT) and Sam Hesling (Carnethy) were 2nd and 3rd and Carnethy took the team prize with Sam, Andrew Fallas and Paul Faulkner (last year’s winner)  
Tessa Hill (HBT) took the ladies prize in 43:12.  
The course record holder from 1997, Bobby Quinn turned up and was 9th in 39.54.His record (33.50) looks safe for a long time!

Dave Hanlon
