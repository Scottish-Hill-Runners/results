_From Stuart Simpson_

16 runners turned out for the first hill race on the highest summit in the Outer Hebrides with Michael Diver arriving, from Clydebank on his bike. Two Iain's, Stewart and MacCorquodale, set a blistering pace at the start of the 2100ft ascent, racing each other all the way to the summit, with only 10s between them at the summit trig point, Iain S proved to be the stronger descender and opened up a comfortable lead by the finish. First female was a local Harris girl, Peigi McKellar with a highly impressive time of 53.44. I would say Peigi's descent was even more impressive than her climb. A big thank you to those that made the trip over from the mainland and those who helped make the race happen. Particular thanks needs to go the Sean Zhein-Stephen, Centre Manager of the Scaladale Centre for helping out with the organisation before, during and after the race and Gavin from Alva( currently in Bermuda as Team Manager for The Western Isles Team at the World Island Games, give them a cheer) whos being living on Lewis for 30 yrs for the wepage on the SRAC website. A day of 'Firsts' on An Cliseam as one of the summit Marshalls had taken his mountain bike with him to the summit, then proceeded to undertake the first bicycle descent of Cliseam.
