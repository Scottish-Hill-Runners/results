Mark Johnston writes to explain the up and coming (or should that be down and going) downhill race series...._

This year sees the launch of the ‘Get a Grip’ Scottish Downhill Running Series run over three consecutive late summer Wednesday evenings on the hills of Central Scotland.

The races follow the format of the original and well established Caerketton Doonhiller, where the downhill racers start from the hill summit at 15 second intervals.

The 2013 ‘Get a Grip’ series will comprise the following races :

Wednesday 28th August : Bishop Downhill, Lomond Hills, Kinneswood Primary, Start 7.00pm

Wednesday 4th September, Nebit Downfall, Ochil Hills, Alva Glen, Start 7.15pm

Wednesday 11th September, Caerketton Doonhiller, Pentland Hills, Start 7.00pm

All doonhillers must register by 6.30pm to allow themselves (and the organisers!) to reach the summit in good time.

The winner in each race scores 1000 points and your score will be the winning time divided by your time then multiplied by 1000. Your best two scores will count and there will be prizes, and much kudos from the Scottish hill running community, for the series winners.

So if you want to end you summer with an adrenaline fuelled frenzy of downhill running, we’ll see you there!
